# nn

### *A ground truth engine for the Indonesian economy*

---

## Why This Exists

For decades, the people who carry the Indonesian economy on their backs — the warung owner, the factory worker, the daily laborer buying sachets because they cannot afford the box — have been told that the numbers are fine. That inflation is manageable. That wages are growing. That the system is working.

They knew it wasn't. They felt it every day. But they had no language for it, no proof, no instrument that could show what they were actually experiencing versus what the official measurements said they should be experiencing.

This project exists because that gap — between what is measured and what is real — is not an accident. It is a structural failure of measurement. And a failure of measurement, left uncorrected long enough, becomes a failure of policy, which becomes a failure of dignity for millions of people.

We are not here to attack anyone. We are here to measure more honestly.

---

## The Core Observation

Official economic measurements in Indonesia were designed for a formal, documented, middle-income economy. They measure what they can see.

But the Indonesian economy lives largely in the spaces official measurement cannot see:

- The warung that doesn't register, doesn't report, doesn't exist in any dataset — but feeds a neighborhood
- The sachet of kopi bought at IDR 1,500 because IDR 10,000 for the box is not available today — paying 4x the per-gram price as a permanent hidden tax on having no savings buffer
- The price held flat for 8 years not because costs didn't rise, but because the warung owner absorbed the pressure until they couldn't — and then the price snapped
- The worker whose wage negotiation is referenced against an inflation number that was never calculated for the things they actually buy

These are not edge cases. This is how the majority lives.

The measurement system sees none of it clearly. And because policy is built on measurement, policy has been systematically miscalibrated — not from malice, but from a ruler that was never built for the thing it was asked to measure.

---

## Vision

A day when any Indonesian — regardless of education, income, or access — can understand what is actually happening to their economy, in their language, through the things they buy every day.

Not through academic papers. Not through policy briefs. Through honest, plain, grounded explanation built on real measurement.

---

## Mission

To build a measuring instrument — not a political argument — that captures the true economic experience of the bottom half of Indonesia, and translates it into terms that any person can understand and verify.

We measure what has not been measured. We explain what has not been explained. We point at no one. We hide nothing.

---

## What We Believe

**The signal is there.** The information about what is really happening in the Indonesian economy exists. It is in the sachet price vs the box price. It is in the cigarette brand people switch to when money gets tight. It is in the length of time a warung held its price before it finally broke. It is in the migration flows, the paylater growth, the shrinking portion sizes. These are not noise. They are the true signal, leaking through the gaps in the official measurement framework.

**The gap is quantifiable.** The difference between what official measurements say and what is actually experienced is not just qualitative. It has a number. It has a history. It accumulated over decades and it can be reconstructed, with honest uncertainty where uncertainty exists, and with confidence where data is strong.

**Understanding precedes change.** We are not a policy advocacy organization. We do not prescribe solutions. We believe that when people — all people, from warung owners to policymakers — can see the true measurement clearly, the conversation about what to do becomes honest in a way it cannot be when the measurement itself is contested or unknown.

**The model must be open.** Every number this project produces must be traceable to its source. Every assumption must be named. Every gap in the data must be visible as a gap, not hidden as a false certainty. Anyone must be able to check our work, challenge our inputs, and improve our methodology. The goal is not to be the authority. The goal is to be the most honest instrument available.

---

## What This Is Not

This is not an attack on BPS, Bank Indonesia, or any government institution. Their measurements are correct for what they measure. We are measuring something they were not designed to measure.

This is not a political project. We have no preferred government, no preferred party, no preferred policy outcome. The measurement should be equally uncomfortable — or equally validating — regardless of who is in power.

This is not an academic project seeking publication or citation. Papers may result from this work, but they are not the goal. The goal is understanding, in the hands of the people who need it most.

This is not finished. It will never be finished. It is an instrument, and instruments are always being calibrated.

## The Initial Ideas That Started This

These are the observations that seeded the project. They are preserved here not as conclusions but as the intuitions that pointed toward a gap worth measuring.

**The warkop price as a compressed signal.** A cup of kopi at a pinggir jalan warung held at IDR 2,000 for nearly 20 years, then moved to IDR 5,000 in under three years. An economist sees delayed inflation adjustment. An engineer sees integrator windup releasing — pressure that accumulated invisibly for two decades, then snapped. These are different diagnoses requiring different responses.

**The feedback loop and its suppressors.** Wages, spending, industry revenue, and prices form a closed loop that should self-regulate. In Indonesia, that loop has been suppressed at multiple points simultaneously — politically, structurally, and through import competition — for so long that the suppression became invisible. The loop didn't break. It was held shut.

**The sachet as a measurement instrument.** The price ratio between a single sachet and the equivalent quantity from a standard pack is a direct measure of the liquidity premium paid by people with daily cash flow rather than monthly income. This ratio is not captured in any official price index. It is a permanent hidden inflation premium paid by those who can least afford it, invisible to the wage-setting mechanisms that are supposed to protect them.

**The NULL signal problem.** Many of the most important economic signals in Indonesia are not noisy or delayed. They simply do not exist in the measurement framework. The warung transaction is not underreported. It is ontologically outside the model. This is a more severe problem than noise — you cannot filter what was never recorded. But it can be reconstructed from proxy signals, with honest uncertainty, using the same techniques engineers use when primary sensors fail.

**The measurement error compounds into policy error.** When the reference signal used to calibrate wage policy is systematically wrong for the population whose wages are being set, the error does not stay small. It accumulates. Each year of miscalibrated adjustment leaves a residual. Integrated over 25 years, this accumulated residual is not a rounding error. It is a structural explanation for why growth statistics can look reasonable while a factory worker cannot afford the box of kopi that their wage increase was theoretically designed to cover.
